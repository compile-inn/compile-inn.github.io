Rosemary

Spring Haircut -- When the flowers dry out, cut the green branches up to a third of their length.

October Haircut -- Cutting the rosemary a bit before the winter will help it against the cold. Remove dryed out branches. Cut on a sunny day to help the healing of the cuts.

Thyme

Harvest -- Can be harvested all year long but its leaves have more taste during flowering.

Haircut -- Ideally, only cut where the branch is still green. Cut less than half of the branch.

Verveina

Harvest -- During summer, in the morning after the dew has dried.

Haircut -- Above a node to help duplication.

Lemon Verveina

Can become very tall - up to 3-4 meters if not cut. Can live up to 20 years if not cut. Sensitive to the cold.

Harvest -- First time around the end of June, beginning of July. Again in August and if the weather allows it around October.

Watering -- When the surface of the dirt is dry.

Haircut -- In spring.

Rusticity -- Will die below -7 degree celcius.
